Ohio Honors Program Reflection

Honestly, I don't think that OHP has overall had a large impact on my college experience, but I do think that the impact it has had has been extremely important. OHP has been the tipping factor for many of my decisions when it comes to experimental learning. When I've needed it, it has been the little nudge that will get me to have a new experience or try something for the first time. There is a chance that I would have done most of the experiences that I have without OHP, but it has made me do them which I think bettered my overall college experience.

One of the more important aspects of experiential learning that I wouldn't have done without OHP was the reflections. I would not have taken the time to reflect on the experiences that I had over college without the push from the program. In retrospect the reflections have been helpful for me to realize all that I have done during an experience. I wouldn't be able to put my journey as a leader in writing without the reflections helping me see every step of that path. Additionally, in general the reflection process has helped me better retain what I've done over college. Without the reflections I would have forgotten much of what I've done.

I've also found the discussion of interdisciplinary learning extremely interesting. We discussed wicked problems, which are problems that require people from multiple disciplines to solve. It was interesting to see how interdisciplinary studies can make you well suited for solving these hard and hyper-specific problems. I've also got the chance to see interdisciplinary learning in action through some of my classes such as the data science course I'm taking. We've been doing analysis on a dataset of mouse DNA which has been extremely interesting. The course plus the discussion of wicked problems has made me see the benefits of interdisciplinary studies.

In the future I plan to use my past experiences to shape my future ones. I'm going to take the important lessons I learned from my leadership experiences to hopefully make a better relationship with my future bosses and possibly those under me if I ever get a leadership position. Seeing what I've learned from being a leader, I think I can use all of that to make the most I can out of any work relationship. I've also already found myself using some of the things I've learned from the talks of ethical research in classes, but in the future, I want to extend this to all jobs I will have. I think it is extremely important to be ethical, and I want to take that with me wherever I end up.

Overall, I've appreciated the opportunity OHP has given me for experiential learning. I think learning by doing is one of the easiest ways to learn and remember what you've learned. I also think the experiences have taught me more than any classroom possibly could have. The push to try new things and seek out new experiences has improved my college experience in many ways, and I would suggest to other college students to also try to seek out new experiences and learn new things while at college.
