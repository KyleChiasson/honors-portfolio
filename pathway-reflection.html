Pathway Reflection

My experiences over my time in OHP have covered all three pathways but the main one that my experiences have focused on is leadership. Many of my experiences have involved multiple pathways and most involved leadership in some capacity. If you would have described the pathways at the start of OHP I would not have thought that leadership would have been my main focus. At the start of college, I didn't see myself as a leader. I can't even point out a particular experience where I started thinking of myself as a leader, but currently after everything I do see myself as one.

My first leadership experience was being president of Ohio University Bobcats for Discovery. This was a newly formed club with members that went to the Discovery Museum here in Athens. This was an interesting experience since I hadn't been in a leadership position before but wanted to help out, and since we were all new I became the first president. I tried my best to help start the club but I don't think I did a great job, but even so I learned a lot from the experience of failure. This led into other leadership experiences such as being a teaching assistant for intro to computer science two.

Being a TA helped me learn about teaching others and that sometimes good leaders are hands off. I think my best takeaway from this experience was how to be hands on and hands off and finding a good balance between those leadership styles.

Another important leadership experience was my time at the OIT Service Desk. I started as a technician but worked my way up to leading the students over my three and a half years there. The most important thing I learned was that you need to be stern as a leader to get results. Now being stern isn't always a necessity but if you are never stern you will need others with external motivation. While working I could easily see that most employees wanted to do the bear minimum, which is a completely normal way to go about a job. This was a problem when my bosses never enforced rules so the bar was set really low. It also made being a leader harder since without consequences I couldn't get people to follow the rules. This is why I say that you have to be some amount of stern when being a leader.

Currently in my final semester of college I am the president of Bobcat Tabletop, a fairly large club on campus. I can now see myself as a successful leader and I attribute that to the cumulative knowledge I've gained from the leadership experiences I've had over the past four years. This experience has also shown me that leadership is not a one-way street. It takes collaboration between leaders and followers to make a good relationship and get things done. Without the great officer boards I've had to support me over the past two years this experience wouldn't have turned out as good as it has.
